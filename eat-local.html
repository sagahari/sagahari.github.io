Case Study

Project Type

UX Design + UI Design

Tools Used

User interviews, Market research, Competitive Analysis, Affinity Map & HMW, Problem Statement, Site Audit, Worst Idea, Crazy 8 ideation, Information Architecture, Sitemap, Lo-fi Wireframes, Mi-fi prototyping & Usability Testing, Brand attributes & moodboard, Style Tile, Hi-fi prototyping, Figma

Duration

15 days

The Challenge

In the last decades, there has been a rise in consciousness on the importance of good nutrition and the responsibility that individuals have to provide themselves with good food. Organic food is not accessible to everyone, being restricted to those who can actually afford it. Supermarket chains and other big companies benefit from the organic food market and conscious customers, but don’t actually solve the situation — they just make the gap and the impact bigger with unsustainable models.

The Project

This is our problematic :How Might We help communities access the seasonal produce of their region, fueling fair and honest relationships between producers and customers while ensuring food safety for all?

Empathize :Empathize:What is Empathize? Considering that you would have traveled in flight, you may have encountered various problems during the journey. When you were about to sleep, suddenly a little baby starts crying and that would have been an annoying situation for most of the passengers on the flight. Some of the passengers might have alienated the parents in the flight. This would have made those parents feel uncomfortable. Do you know what Empathize means here? A similar kind of incident happened when I was traveling from Europe to Asia on a flight. A 3-year-old boy winkled from his seat and started walking towards the baby and gave his pacifier to the baby. The toddler had a beautiful heart! This is the true meaning of Empathizing.

The toddler understood the pain of that baby and acted quickly to stop the baby from crying by giving his pacifier…. this is called Design Thinking in the UX design world. A true UX designer has to go deep into the consumer’s emotions and pain to get perfect data. What does the consumer want?

Define :Nowadays, most of us are concerned with the health issues and liability of our planet. So during the covid pandemic, many of us changed our diet and what we consume. We constantly felt the need to find a way to live in more peaceful life. In these stressful times, it is important for many of us to reconnect in this world, where we are to help each other, to improve our health, and more than that, to impact our society and make a difference through our lifestyle.

Where to find organic food products?

Where to buy local and seasonal food?

Is it really worth it?

Can we purchase with our budget?

However, I was able to find some pain points :

  • She does not know the seasonal products
  • She doesn’t have time to go somewhere else apart from the supermarket, and she doesn’t know where she can buy better quality products
  • She doesn’t have enough information concerning the products (origin, etc.)
  • Pauline manages her budget very well and she thinks that buying seasonal and organic products is more expensive

I decided to focus on a MVP, to work on a version of the Application with just enough features to be usable by early customers. My main focus was to work on the information architecture and UX writing.
