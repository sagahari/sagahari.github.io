u tap

Case Study

Project Type

UX Design + UI Design

Tools Used

User interviews, Market research, Competitive Analysis, Affinity Map & HMW, Problem Statement, Site Audit, Worst Idea, Crazy 8 ideation, Information Architecture, Sitemap, Lo-fi Wireframes, Mi-fi prototyping & Usability Testing, Brand attributes & moodboard, Style Tile, Hi-fi prototyping, Figma

Duration

14 days

The Challenge

our mission is to design a desktop website for a local professional business. In this project, we’re focusing our efforts on undoubtedly organizing the information to be more effective for both customers and providers. Also, we want to tackle Interaction design patterns to make the exchange as streamlined as possible. We will use most of the research time for bench-marking and market research as well as developing a sense of professional best practices and rules of thumb you can always rely on.

The Project

This is our problematic :Curious extroverts need a contactless way to exchange contact securely and have all information accessible.

Empathize :U TAP is the next-generation digital business card. It uses NFC technology to make sharing contact information as easy as using Apple Pay. U TAP products allow you to share contact info, social media, payment platforms, book an appointment, music, and more with just a tap.

“Once a new technology rolls over you, if you’re not part of the steamroller, you’re part of the road.”

Stewart Brand, Writer

Define :We believe that offering a fast, contactless and secure way of exchanging contacts for people who want to save time will achieve reassurance of sharing data and information in a secure way. We will know we are right if the user rates and gives positive reviews on the store.?

The purpose of the u Tap website is to inform users about the products offered at the e-shop as well as provide information about NFC technology and its benefits. The website also tries to promote consumer action by letting them know what is accessible. As a result, we must take the following steps:

Create a one-page multi-page website with all the necessary information about the product. This will include e-shop, custom NFC tags, compatibility testing, user account, social media profiles and customer support with the refund policy.

However, I was able to find some pain points…

  • Lack of information.
  • Don’t know what all features can be added in NFC Tags.
  • User doesn’t have enough information concerning the products (origin, etc.)

Worried about security and data breach,I decided to focus on a MVP, to work on a version of the Application with just enough features to be usable by early customers.

My main focus was to work on information architecture and UX writing.
