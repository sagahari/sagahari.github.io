Case Study

Project Type

UX Design + UI Design

Tools Used

User interviews, Market research, Competitive Analysis, Affinity Map & HMW, Problem Statement, Site Audit, Worst Idea, Crazy 8 ideation, Information Architecture, Sitemap, Lo-fi Wireframes, Mi-fi prototyping & Usability Testing, Brand attributes & moodboard, Style Tile, Hi-fi prototyping, Figma

Duration

15 days

The Challenge

The National Wellness Institute wants designers to re-imagine how people can adopt and maintain a routine that enhances their well-being.

Tool can be focused in any category that relates to personal well being, such as (but not limited to): exercise and fitness, eating/diet, meditation, time management, etc.

The only requirement is that it tracks the user’s progress and pushes them to commit to a healthier lifestyle.

The Project

This is our problematic :the client wants the prototype to focus on the main user flow that will be the defining factor of the user experience, and not worry about the following:

  • Login
  • Sign-up
  • Account / User Management

Empathize :There were 580,530 teen pregnancies among women aged 18–19 years in the United States. This figure had decreased to 235,810 (2019)

prevent and help them in an effective way we need a solution to avoid the fear and misconception of pregnancy stories and emotions.

deep into the emotions of the user!

To whom we are creating this application :

  • user who likes to be pregnant in future
  • The user is pregnant for three months
  • And the user who is pregnant and need guidance after birth

These key points guide us to travel in the proper course in creation of our application.

Define :Which feature/functionality to include is open for proposals from the designer.

As this is an MVP for a competition, the app should not try to solve all of the wellness issues people have at the same time. Each design should focus on:

ONE aspect of wellness that the designer consider important (backed up by user research)

The corresponding prototype should consist of:

  1. The profile and goals.
  2. The user flow for the main feature of the application should be define the research what we have done and execute the Goals of wellness application.

However, I was able to find some pain points…

  • So longer for having medical appointments.
  • Feels alone when faced with worries.
  • Lack of information about food intake and to avoid.
  • Afraid about the unknown details.

I decided to focus on a MVP, to work on a version of the Application with just enough features to be usable by early customers.

My main focus was to work on information architecture and UX writing.
